# Astrology Interpretation Framework

## Interpretation layers

Use these layers in order when enough chart data is available.

1. Data integrity
   - What chart data is present?
   - Is exact birth time available?
   - Are zodiac and house systems known?
   - Which placements are uncertain?

2. Core orientation
   - Sun: vitality, identity, creative center.
   - Moon: emotional needs, instincts, inner life.
   - Ascendant: presentation, embodiment, life approach.
   - Chart ruler: how the Ascendant's ruler themes move through the chart.

3. Personal style
   - Mercury: mind, language, learning, communication.
   - Venus: desire, relational style, values, aesthetics.
   - Mars: drive, conflict style, assertion, pursuit.

4. Growth and structure
   - Jupiter: expansion, faith, opportunity, meaning.
   - Saturn: discipline, boundaries, responsibility, maturation.
   - Nodes: developmental direction when requested or supplied.
   - Chiron: sensitive learning themes when requested or supplied.

5. Collective background
   - Uranus, Neptune, Pluto as generational themes, made personal through houses, aspects, or angles.

6. Houses and life areas
   - Use houses only when birth time or chart table supports them.
   - Connect house topics with sign style and planetary function.

7. Aspects and patterns
   - Prioritize conjunctions, oppositions, squares, trines, and sextiles.
   - Emphasize tight or repeated patterns.
   - Interpret tensions as dynamic rather than bad.

8. Element and modality balance
   - Fire: initiative, vitality, inspiration.
   - Earth: practicality, embodiment, stability.
   - Air: thought, connection, abstraction.
   - Water: emotion, intuition, sensitivity.
   - Cardinal: initiation.
   - Fixed: continuity.
   - Mutable: adaptation.

9. Synthesis
   - Identify repeated themes across signs, houses, aspects, and chart rulers.
   - Name strengths, tensions, needs, and growth invitations.
   - Include practical reflection prompts.

## Synthesis pattern

Use this pattern for each major theme:

```text
Theme: [plain-language theme]
Chart indicators: [placements/aspects used]
How it may show up: [grounded description]
Strength: [empowering expression]
Growth edge: [nonjudgmental challenge]
Reflection prompt: [question]
```

## Avoiding overreach

Do not infer:

- Diagnoses.
- Certain life events.
- Exact timing without valid transit/progression data.
- Moral character.
- Trauma history.
- Sexuality.
- Fertility or pregnancy outcomes.
- Death or disaster.
- Professional suitability, creditworthiness, safety risk, or legal status.
